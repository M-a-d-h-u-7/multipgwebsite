Where It Began

OBSIDIAN started in a 400 sq ft studio in Indiranagar, Bengaluru. Kiran Nair, a former textile designer, grew tired of watching fast fashion cannibalise the craft she'd spent years studying. She decided to build something different — something slow, intentional, and permanent.

The first collection was twelve pieces. Twelve. All in the same palette of near-blacks and warm neutrals. All sold out in three days through word of mouth alone.

What We Stand For

Our Values

✦

Permanence Over Trend

We design against the calendar. No spring/summer collections, no limited-time colours that vanish. Every OBSIDIAN piece is designed to be worn a decade from now.

◈

Craft Over Speed

We work with a small network of artisan workshops in Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Every partnership is long-term, every wage above industry standard, every process audited annually.

⟡

Material Honesty

Our fabrics are organic, recycled, or upcycled — and we tell you exactly what each piece is made of, where it came from, and what to do with it when you're done.

◇

Radical Restraint

We release fewer than 30 styles per year. We don't do sales. We don't do collaborations. We do one thing and we do it as well as we possibly can.

▲

Repair, Not Replace

Every OBSIDIAN garment comes with a lifetime repair guarantee. Send it back, we'll fix it. We believe the most sustainable garment is the one you already own.

○

End-of-Life Thinking

When a garment truly can't be repaired, we take it back. We compost what's compostable, recycle what's recyclable, and learn from what we can't handle yet.
